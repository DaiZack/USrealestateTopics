Pending home sales in August dropped a seasonally adjusted 6.5% from July, as tighter restrictions on mortgage lending made it tougher for some would-be buyers to get financing.

The decline, which came on the heels of a revised 10.7% drop in July, was steeper than expected. Pending home sales, as tracked by the National Association of Realtors, are now at their lowest since the data were first published in 2001.

Pending home sales are the number of home-sale contracts signed, but which haven't yet closed, and serve as a leading indicator of existing-home sales. But because they don't reflect the rising number of cancellations, the figures may overstate the probable number of existing-home sales, which are based on sales that have closed. The latest data suggest that sales of existing, or previously owned, homes have yet to hit bottom. Those figures are to be released Oct. 24.

Pending home sales in August dropped 21.5% from a year earlier, reflecting weakness nationwide. Sales in the West, heavily influenced by California, fell 27.1%. Sales in the South fell 21.3%, while those in the Northeast declined 18.3% and Midwest sales were down 18%.
